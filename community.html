Home Fellowship

The Church in Your Home

"And they continued steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine and fellowship, in the breaking of bread, and in prayers... breaking bread from house to house, they ate their food with gladness and simplicity of heart."
Acts 2:42, 46 (NKJV)

In the New Testament, the church gathered in homes. Christians are God's household and His church whenever we come together. You don't need a building or a program — you need believers, the Word, and a willingness to love one another.

Three Goals of Home Fellowship

Study & Apply the Word

Share the Word together, discuss its meaning, and apply it. The secret isn't just knowing God's Word — it's obeying it.

Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 · John 14:23 · James 1:22

Supportive Fellowship

Provide an environment where believers can fellowship in a supportive atmosphere — discipling one another in personal growth.

Matthew 28:19 · Hebrews 10:24 · Ephesians 4:12

Worship & Pray Together

Enjoy worshipping together through songs of praise, and breaking bread together.

Ephesians 5:18-21 · Colossians 3:16 · Acts 2:46

Home Fellowship Format

1

7:30 PM — Opening Prayer & Announcements

Begin with prayer and share relevant information with the group.

2

7:35 PM — Singing & Praise

Lift your voices in worship and praise to the Lord.

3

8:00 PM — Discussion & Application

The heart of the fellowship — explore Scripture together and discuss how to live it out.

4

8:50 PM — Sharing & Prayer in Small Groups

Break into smaller groups for deeper sharing and intercession.

5

9:30 PM — Light Refreshments & Fellowship

Enjoy fellowship and refreshments as the meeting concludes.

Servants, Not Leaders

The facilitator's role is to serve, not to lead. Six essential tips:

Pray for the Meeting

Intercede for those attending and the Holy Spirit's guidance throughout.

Prepare the Study

Know the material intimately so you can guide discussion effectively.

Facilitate Don't Dominate

Ask questions, listen to others, and let the group discover insights together.

Follow Up with People

Connect with members between meetings to encourage and support them.

Multiply by Dividing

When your group grows, help establish new groups to keep them relational.

Use Your Gift

Let your unique abilities shape how you serve the fellowship.
