Home Fellowship

The Church in Your Home

"And they continued steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine and fellowship, in the breaking of bread, and in prayers... breaking bread from house to house, they ate their food with gladness and simplicity of heart."

— Acts 2:42, 46 (NKJV)

In the New Testament, the church gathered in homes. Christians are God's household and His church whenever we come together. You don't need a building or a program — you need believers, the Word, and a willingness to love one another. That's what Home Fellowship is.

The Vision

Three Goals of Home Fellowship

The early church was built on this — the pure teaching of God's Word and the home fellowship of believers. Everything we do flows from these goals.

Study & Apply the Word

Share the Word together, discuss its meaning, and apply it to your life. The secret isn't just knowing God's Word — it's obeying it. We must not only be taught, but we must apply the Word to our personal lives and obey His commands.

Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 · John 14:23 · James 1:22

Supportive Fellowship

Provide an environment where believers can fellowship in a supportive atmosphere — discipling one another in personal growth, encouraging obedience and servanthood, and equipping each other for the work of ministry.

Matthew 28:19 · Hebrews 10:24 · Ephesians 4:12

Worship & Pray Together

Enjoy worshipping together through songs of praise, and breaking bread together — whether a formal communion, a simple snack, or a shared meal. Worship isn't a performance. It's how we prepare our hearts to receive.

Ephesians 5:18-21 · Colossians 3:16 · Acts 2:46

The Format

How a Home Fellowship Works

This isn't rigid — the Holy Spirit may shift things around. But having a plan keeps the time focused and fruitful. Here's a suggested two-hour format.

1
7:30 PM

Opening Prayer & Announcements

Begin by asking God for revelation, light, and application of the Word to your lives. A prayer focused on being encouraged, enriched, and challenged. Set the tone — this is about Him, not us.

2
7:35 PM

Singing & Praise

Worship prepares our hearts and minds to receive God's Word. Use simple songs people can join in on. This isn't a concert — it's a posture. Even 10 minutes of genuine worship shifts the atmosphere.

3
8:00 PM

Discussion & Application

This is the heart of the fellowship. Follow along with a teaching from StayTheWay or study a book of the Bible together. The leader isn't the preacher — they're the facilitator. Keep the application of God's Word to daily life front and center. Interactive study holds attention and gets people digging into the Word for themselves.

4
8:50 PM

Sharing & Prayer in Small Groups

Break into groups of 2-5 people. Share prayer requests, celebrate answered prayers, bear one another's burdens, and pray together. This is where real relationship happens. In a group of 20, you get 2 minutes each. In a group of 4, you get 10 minutes. That's where it becomes real.

5
9:30 PM

Light Refreshments & Fellowship

Break bread together. It can be simple — coffee and a snack. This is where the informal conversations happen, where people connect, and where community actually forms. Rotate who brings refreshments so no one feels burdened.

The Heart

Servants, Not Leaders

Jesus trained no leaders — only servants. The world says leadership is about being in charge. Jesus flipped it. A Home Fellowship servant is not someone who directs people — they serve God's people, and God will guide them.

Pray for the Meeting

Come prepared spiritually. The shepherd prays for the flock during the week — not just at the meeting.

Prepare the Study

Follow a systematic book-by-book study or use a StayTheWay teaching. Everyone can study ahead and come ready to discuss.

Facilitate, Don't Dominate

Sit in a circle, not in a pulpit arrangement. Keep your own comments to 5-10 minutes. Let the group discuss and discover together.

Follow Up with People

If someone hasn't shown up for a while, reach out. Not to pressure them to attend — but because you care. That's what a shepherd does.

Multiply by Dividing

When a group grows beyond 15-20 people, split it. Intimacy is lost in a crowd. The early church multiplied by dividing — and so should we.

Use Your Gift

God has given every believer at least one spiritual gift for the building up of the body. The fellowship is the place to discover it, practice it, and grow in it.

Get Started

How to Start a Home Fellowship

You don't need a seminary degree. You need a heart for God, a willingness to serve, and a living room.

1

Pray About It

The first thing necessary is a heart for God. Nothing replaces a genuine desire to serve. Ask God to show you the right people to invite and the right time to begin.

2

Pick a Study

Choose a book of the Bible to go through together, or use a StayTheWay teaching series. Having a consistent, verse-by-verse plan keeps the group anchored in the Word — not drifting into topical rabbit holes.

3

Invite Believers Near You

Gather believers as close to home as possible — people who love Jesus and want to grow. Ideally hosted by a committed couple. Ask for a 6-month time commitment upfront so the group can develop real trust.

4

Set a Time & Keep It

Most groups meet weekly for about two hours. Pick a time, start on time, and end on time. Consistency builds trust and shows respect for people's lives and families.

5

Use StayTheWay Resources

Watch a teaching together, use the handouts for discussion, take the quiz during the week. Everything you need for a structured, Word-centered Home Fellowship is already here — and it's free.
