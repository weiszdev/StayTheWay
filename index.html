Frameworks

Tools for thinking through pain, patterns, and purpose.

Framework

R3 Framework

Recognize. Reframe. Respond.

▾

Recognize — Name what's happening. What triggered you? What are you feeling?

Reframe — Challenge the narrative. What's actually true? What would the Holy Spirit say?

Respond — Choose your next move with intention, not reaction.

Model

BENR Model

Beliefs. Emotions. Narratives. Responses.

▾

Beliefs — What do you believe about yourself, God, and your situation?

Emotions — What are you feeling? Name it without judgment.

Narratives — What story are you telling yourself? Is it true?

Responses — How have you been responding? How could you respond differently?

Tool

Crisis Clarity

Decision-making in chaos.

▾

When everything feels urgent and overwhelming, Crisis Clarity helps you slow down and decide what actually needs your attention right now.

Is anyone in immediate physical danger?

Yes — Someone is at risk right now
No — It's emotional/situational, not physical

Does this need to be resolved today?

Yes — There's a real deadline
No — It feels urgent but can wait

Is this your decision to make?

Yes — This is mine to own
No — I'm carrying someone else's burden
Education

Trauma Bond

Understanding relational patterns.

▾

Trauma bonds are deep emotional attachments formed through cycles of abuse and intermittent reinforcement. Understanding these patterns is the first step to breaking free.

About Jonathan

I'm not a therapist. I'm not a pastor. I'm a husband and father who learned to think through pain.

After surviving cancer, recovering from a traumatic brain injury, and navigating a faith journey that broke everything I thought I knew — I found a way through. Not around. Through.

Now I help others do the same. Not with clinical advice, but with frameworks, faith, and the kind of honesty that only comes from lived experience.

Stay The Way
