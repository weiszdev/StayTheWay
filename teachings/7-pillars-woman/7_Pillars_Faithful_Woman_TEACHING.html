7PILLARS

of a Faithful Woman

Toward Her Husband

KEY VERSE — FOUNDATION OF ALL 7 PILLARS
“My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me.”
— Jesus, John 10:27 (NKJV)
Led by the Holy Spirit  •  April 2026
Teaching principle. A woman is faithful to God independently of her husband's response. She hears the Shepherd's voice first — and out of that, she honors, builds, and blesses, regardless of whether she is honored, built, or blessed in return. Every pillar stands independent of anyone else's faithfulness.
1
She Hears Her Shepherd's Voice
John 10:27 (NKJV)
“My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me.”
CONTRAST Eve — She listened to another voice in the garden instead of the voice of her Shepherd.
KEY VERSE ECHO Before she is a wife, she is a sheep of His pasture. Every other pillar rises only as high as this one.
2
She Honors and Reverences Her Husband
Ephesians 5:33 (NKJV)
“Nevertheless let each one of you in particular so love his own wife as himself, and let the wife see that she respects her husband.”
CONTRAST Michal — She despised David in her heart when he worshiped the Lord freely.
KEY VERSE ECHO When she hears the Shepherd first, she can honor her husband even when he is imperfect — because her respect flows from Christ, not from his performance.
3
She Builds Her House, Never Tears It Down
Proverbs 14:1 (NKJV)
“The wise woman builds her house, but the foolish pulls it down with her hands.”
CONTRAST Jezebel — She used her voice and hands to pull Ahab and her house into ruin.
KEY VERSE ECHO She follows the Shepherd's voice, so her hands build. She does not use words or will to dismantle what God is raising up.
4
She Adorns the Hidden Heart — Gentle and Quiet Spirit
1 Peter 3:3–4 (NKJV)
“Do not let your adornment be merely outward—arranging the hair, wearing gold, or putting on fine apparel— rather let it be the hidden person of the heart, with the incorruptible beauty of a gentle and quiet spirit, which is very precious in the sight of God.”
CONTRAST Sapphira — She agreed with her husband in hidden deception instead of in hidden holiness.
KEY VERSE ECHO A heart tuned to the Shepherd becomes quiet. Quietness is not silence — it is a soul that stopped striving because it already heard Him.
5
She Wins Him Without a Word
1 Peter 3:1–2 (NKJV)
“Wives, likewise, be submissive to your own husbands, that even if some do not obey the word, they, without a word, may be won by the conduct of their wives, when they observe your chaste conduct accompanied by fear.”
CONTRAST Job's wife — In his suffering she said, “Curse God and die.” She preached despair instead of living hope.
KEY VERSE ECHO When she hears the Shepherd clearly, she does not need to preach her husband home. Her conduct becomes the sermon.
6
She Does Him Good All the Days of Her Life
Proverbs 31:10–12 (NKJV)
“Who can find a virtuous wife? For her worth is far above rubies. The heart of her husband safely trusts her; so he will have no lack of gain. She does him good and not evil all the days of her life.”
CONTRAST Delilah — She studied her man to destroy him, not to bless him.
KEY VERSE ECHO A woman who hears the Shepherd becomes safe ground for her husband's heart. Not perfect ground — safe ground.
7
She Looks Forward, Never Back
Luke 17:32  •  Philippians 3:13–14 (NKJV)
“Remember Lot's wife.”

“Forgetting those things which are behind and reaching forward to those things which are ahead, I press toward the goal for the prize of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus.”
CONTRAST Lot's wife — She was delivered from Sodom, but her heart stayed behind. She looked back, and she was lost.
KEY VERSE ECHO Sheep that hear His voice do not glance back at old pastures. They follow.
WRAP-UP

The Shepherd's Promise
to the Woman Who Hears

“My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me. And I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish; neither shall anyone snatch them out of My hand.”
— John 10:27–28 (NKJV)
HER CALL TODAY Hear His voice first.
Let the seven pillars rise.
Be faithful to God—independent of any response.
Companion scripture  •  Ephesians 5:33 (NKJV) …let the wife see that she respects her husband.
